    …. The idiosyncratic argument of that epilogue is that history — the discipline — is doomed to fail. We want to explain things — say the 2008 crash — in terms of “great” (not necessarily “good”) people, or again of social and economic forces, or again of ideas (thus, perhaps, Allan Greenspan or Dodd Frank or neoliberal financialization). But all such explanations are bad explanations, Tolstoy thinks. Reality defies them. That’s not to say there are no explanations. There are: we just can’t figure them out, because they’re so massively complex. History unfolds according to laws too complex for humans to grasp, and even the best of us, the “great people” of history, are subject to these laws.
