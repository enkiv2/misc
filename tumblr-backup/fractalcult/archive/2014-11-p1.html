Fun with self-deception

You’re lying to yourself. We all are. But hey, fuck it, that’s life.

Alright, it’s not life. But it’s matter.

Seriously.

Do you know what a galaxy is made of? 99 point something percent of it is empty space. I mean, we live on the fraction of a percent, but still, it’s like throwing a couple of pins into an empty ballroom.

But you know what’s really similar to a galaxy? An atom.

Down to the amount of empty space between the stuff in the middle (protons, neutrons) and the floaty bits around the edge (electrons).

Now this isn’t a science lesson, so I’m not going to get too bogged down in the numbers. I’m glossing over some details, but the point remains valid. And atoms are mostly nothing. Like galaxies. From here, a galaxy sure as hell looks like a whole lot of stuff, but if you get up close (like on a planet?) you see a whole lot of nothing between little bits of something.

Likewise, from the surface of a planet, stuff looks solid, but it’s 99 point something percent nothing.

Everything big maps to smaller stuff. The known universe looks like a brain cell. A galaxy looks like an atom. And if you keep going, with that 99 point whatever percent nothing?

The amount of stuff that isn’t nothing - that is actually matter - gets closer and closer to 0. And as we get more data, we start figuring out… well, we start figuring out that “nothing” might be something we can’t wrap our heads around right now.

Because if all that nothing really is nothing, then it starts looking like matter is nothing pretending really hard that it isn’t nothing.

So I think my point is that sometimes it takes a few nonsensical delusions to get you through the very real possibility that you’re a whole lot of nothing that kept going through a lot of bullshitting. The problem, I think, is not the delusion. The problem is when you forget that the delusion is a delusion. There’s not a pretty good chance you’re all the delusion of some demented nothing that decided it was going to pretend to not be nothing, just to be a nonconformist. 

So maybe we are all just delusional void pretending it’s matter for a little while. Either way, don’t worry so much, don’t be a jerk, and you’re good.

I knew a motivational speaker who said the same thing, in fewer words. She told me that you don’t have to know exactly what you’re doing to get what you want out of life. You just need a rough goal, a good first step, and the will to follow it through no matter what. I mean, I think it’s implied you need to know you don’t always know the next step. If you don’t and you’re pretending you do, you just get all anxious and angsty, or you get defensive and shitty - maybe both. A lot can go foul when you forget you’re just pretending to be something nobody really is - that is to say, pretending to have your shit very together.

Know what I do when I get anxious and angsty? I pull out a physics book, and I start thinking about how it’s possible - incredibly unlikely, mind, but possible - that all matter is just void trying to pretend otherwise. Sure, maybe my delusion sounds weird to you, but over a beer, that thought just makes me laugh, every time.

Cheers,

Saint Amir Zetathustra, Heretic
